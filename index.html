Why should teens eat a balanced,
nutrient-rich diet?

  1. Physical Growth and Aging

    2. A nutrient-rich diet takes a huge role in supporting mental and physical growth, especially for children aged 12-18. This is because many children experience growth spurts at this age, using up a lot of the body's resources. As a result, adolescents may get hungry more often and may require a more balanced and nutrient-rich diet.


  2. Strong Immunity

    3. It is important to obtain a healthy immunity system, as this will help fight infection against nasty diseases. This means that if someone has a strong immune system, they will get sick less often, which means that they won't have to lie in bed all day eating bitter soup. Fotunately, though, a strong immune system can be obtained through a balanced, nutrient-rich diet! Hooray!


  3. Healthy eating habits

    4. It's crucial to get into good eating habits as a child. As you grow up, health problems such as diabetes and obesity become more common. To prevent being a victim of such health issues, adolescents must eat a good diet consisting of all the nutrients they need. Research has shown that older people generally have less tastebuds than children. This means that they might start to eat stronger and unhealthier foods such as chocolate, burgers, pizza, etc just so they can get a better taste of their food. To prevent yourself from eating unhealthy foods, start developing good eating habits now. Believe me, it's much easier now than it will be when you're older!


  4. Organ and Tissue Development

    5. During the age of 14 (adolescence), organs, such as the brain, heart and lungs, grow and expand at the fastest rate in a person's life. This means that having a nutritional diet consisting of all the vital nutrients is important to avoid malnutrition. Types of malnutrition include growth disorder, which is rare, but it can happen. In this disease, a person's organs, bones and muscles don't grow properly, making them way shorter than a normal person. This is why it is vital to have a nutrient-rich diet.


  5. Emotional Health

    6. Having a good, well-balanced diet will not only affect you physically, but emotionally too! Research has shown that people who lack a particular nutrient called Vitamin D often experience symtoms such as mood swings, depression and fatigue. So, next time you're about to eat something unhealthy, think about the affect it will have on your emotions!
